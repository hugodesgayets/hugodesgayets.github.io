Design a system

Mapstr’s old logotype and app icon had gone practically unchanged since the company’s creation and it was more than time for a refresh. The weird color, the hash icon plus the lack of typographic effort — using a default system font — clearly made the brand system feel outdated.

Icon

To visually empower the brand, I put emphasis on the marker — the core symbol of the product experience — Its design appeared in a more geometric yet bolder aspect, and could easily stood up at small size context. I also warmed up the brand color, to remain friendly and also highlight the tech-savvy-side of the product.

Logotype

Alongside the icon development, I designed a custom sans-serif wordmark and provided technical specs including color guides, spacing, and clearance rules.

Building a platform

Mapstr aims to be the default platform for all your places — its model should be flexible, trustworthy and secure. As a platform, users should be able to manage their data how they want. Connected with your apps

As a platform, we needed to communicate with apps and external data providers.
I designed and shipped a module that let users import places from different APIs — parsing retrieved geopoints, and converting them into places with valid content informations. Local source files such as .csv or .klm were also supported.
To build up the platform experience, users could also browse recent deleted data through a backup storage settings page.

Designed for the inner circle

Introducing users account allowed users to interact and share within the app was a milestone step — it massively increased acquisition — which at that moment, represented a bottom line lever to our company’s business growth.
Following research outcomes we defined a simple account model architecture. I was reponsible for the complete UX workflow, from the first run experience to the design, tests and implementation of the profile features.
